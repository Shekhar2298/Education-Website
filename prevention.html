STI Prevention & Information

Understanding sexually transmitted infections and how to prevent them

Common STIs

Information about frequently encountered sexually transmitted infections.

  • Chlamydia: Bacterial infection, often asymptomatic
  • Gonorrhea: Bacterial infection, can cause serious complications
  • HIV: Viral infection affecting immune system
  • HPV: Common virus, some strains can cause cancer
  • Herpes: Viral infection causing sores
  • Syphilis: Bacterial infection with multiple stages

Prevention Strategies

Effective ways to reduce STI transmission risk.

  • Consistent condom use
  • Regular STI testing
  • Vaccinations (HPV, Hepatitis B)
  • Mutual monogamy
  • Open communication with partners
  • Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP) for HIV

Emergency Contraception

Options for preventing pregnancy after unprotected sex

Emergency Contraception Pills

Also known as "morning after pills."

  • Levonorgestrel pills: Effective up to 72 hours
  • Ulipristal acetate: Effective up to 120 hours
  • Availability: Over-the-counter or prescription
  • Effectiveness: Higher when taken sooner

Important: Emergency contraception prevents pregnancy but does not protect against STIs.

Copper IUD

The most effective form of emergency contraception.

  • Effectiveness: Over 99%
  • Timeframe: Up to 5 days after unprotected sex
  • Additional benefit: Provides ongoing contraception
  • Requires: Healthcare provider insertion
